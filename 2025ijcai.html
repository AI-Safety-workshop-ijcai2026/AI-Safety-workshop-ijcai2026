IJCAI 2025 Deepfake Workshop

Welcome to the Deepfake Detection, Localization, and Interpretability Workshop held at IJCAI 2025 in Montreal/Guangzhou on August 16, 2025.

This was our inaugural workshop addressing AI-generated content security challenges, which laid the foundation for the current IJCAI-ECAI 2026 workshop.

Official Website: https://deepfake-workshop-ijcai2025.github.io/main/

Workshop Overview

The IJCAI 2025 workshop focused on new security challenges brought about by the rapid development of AI-generated content technology. As Deepfake technology expanded from single face synthesis to multimodal content generation, the reliability of existing detection methods faced severe challenges in real complex scenarios.

Key Focus Areas

  • Multimodal Deepfake Detection: Addressing deepfake detection across text, image, audio, and video modalities
  • Interpretable Detection Methods: Developing methods that provide visual evidence chains for law enforcement agencies
  • LLM/VLM-based Interpretability: Leveraging large language and vision-language models for explainable detection
  • Cross-scenario Generalization: Building robust detection systems for diverse real-world scenarios

Workshop Sessions

The workshop featured a comprehensive program including:

  • Keynote Presentations: Leading researchers shared insights on deepfake detection and AI safety
  • Technical Paper Sessions: Oral presentations of cutting-edge research
  • Competition Results: Analysis and awards for the deepfake detection challenge
  • Best Paper Awards: Recognition of exceptional research contributions

Competition Tracks

The IJCAI 2025 workshop hosted a deepfake detection competition with the following awards:

  • 1st Place: Certificate + 1000 USD
  • 2nd Place: Certificate + 500 USD
  • 3rd Place: Certificate + 300 USD
  • Best Paper: Certificate + 1000 USD

Research Topics

The workshop covered a broad range of research topics including:

  • Detection Techniques: Multi-scene and multi-modal deepfake detection, safety risk content identification
  • Interpretable Methods: Weakly-supervised localization, LLM/VLM-based reasoning frameworks, scalable detection agents
  • Adversarial Attack and Defense: Attack techniques, risk-trigger mechanisms, robust defense strategies for generative models
  • Face Spoofing Detection: Threat analysis, physical attack detection, joint physical-digital attack detection
  • Datasets and Evaluation: Multi-modal spatiotemporal datasets, safety risk assessment, standardized protocols

Connection to IJCAI-ECAI 2026

The success of IJCAI 2025 workshop inspired us to continue this important work at IJCAI-ECAI 2026 in Bremen. Our 2026 workshop, titled "DDL 2.0: AIGC Safety from Dual Perspectives of Model and Content", builds upon the foundation laid in 2025 and expands the scope to include:

  • Model-level security defenses as the "first line of defense"
  • Content-level identification as the "last line of defense"
  • End-to-end collaborative analysis from generative models to emitted content
  • Systematic security frameworks spanning the full AIGC lifecycle

We invite you to explore both workshops and join us in advancing AI safety research!
