Track 3 Overview

Large Generative Models (LGMs) deliver strong performance in perception and generation, but pose nontrivial security risks. With language prompts, LLMs and T2I models can yield harmful or policy-violating outputs; in MLLMs, vision-language interactions amplify threats, and evolving attacks continue to bypass defenses.

Therefore, we present the Safety Attack-and-defense for Generative Large Models Challenge that aims to systematically reveal and evaluate safety vulnerabilities of LGMs across diverse attack scenarios.

More details about the competition can be found in our Submission ID 11 to the Competitions Track of IJCAI-ECAI 2026, where we present a comprehensive proposal.

Track Focus Areas

Key Security Challenges

  • Language Prompt Attacks: LLMs and T2I models can yield harmful or policy-violating outputs through carefully crafted language prompts.
  • Vision-Language Interaction Threats: In MLLMs, vision-language interactions amplify security threats.
  • Evolving Attack Methods: Advanced attacks continue to bypass existing defense mechanisms.
  • Safety Vulnerability Assessment: Systematic evaluation of LGM safety across diverse attack scenarios.

Research Topics

  • Jailbreak attacks and defenses for large language models
  • Prompt injection and adversarial attacks
  • Safety alignment and robustness evaluation
  • Multi-modal attack strategies and countermeasures
  • Red-teaming methodologies for generative models
  • Policy violation detection and prevention

Awards and Recognition

  • Monetary prizes: Substantial monetary awards will be granted to top-performing teams.
  • Academic recognition: Exceptional solutions will be invited for presentation at the IJCAI.

Additional Information

This track focuses on the security challenges of generative foundation models from both attack and defense perspectives. Participants will develop and evaluate methods to identify vulnerabilities and create robust defense mechanisms against various attack vectors targeting large generative models.
